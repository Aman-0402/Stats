Part 1: Binomial Distribution

üéØ What is Binomial Distribution?

A Binomial distribution models the probability of getting exactly x successes in n independent trials, where each trial has only two outcomes:

  • Success (‚úîÔ∏è)

‚úÖ Conditions for Binomial Distribution

Part 2: Poisson Distribution

What is Poisson Distribution?

Poisson distribution gives the probability of a given number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space, when events occur:

  • randomly
  • independently
  • at a constant average rate

üëâ It answers:

"How many times will an event occur in a fixed interval?"

‚úÖ Conditions for Poisson Distribution

  1. Events occur independently
  2. Average rate (Œª) is constant
  3. Two events cannot occur at the same instant
  4. Probability of more than one event in a very small interval ‚âà 0

Part 3: Continuous Probability Distribution ‚Äì Normal Distribution

What is a Continuous Probability Distribution?

A continuous distribution:

  • Deals with continuous values
  • Probability is measured over an interval
  • Probability at a single point = 0
üëâ Example: height, weight, time, marks, errors

üéØ What is Normal Distribution?

A Normal distribution is a symmetrical, bell-shaped probability distribution where:

  • Most values cluster around the mean
  • Extreme values are rare
  • Left and right sides are mirror images

It is also called:

  • Gaussian Distribution
  • Bell Curve

üîë Key Characteristics

  1. Mean = Median = Mode
  2. Curve is perfectly symmetric
  3. Total area under curve = 1
  4. Defined by two parameters:
    • Mean (Œº)
    • Standard deviation (œÉ)
