Descriptive vs. Inferential Statistics

Statistics has two main branches:

  • Descriptive Statistics ‚Üí What does the data show?
  • Inferential Statistics ‚Üí What can we conclude about the population from a sample?

Why is it Important?

üìò Statistics: Basic classification of the subject

üíª Data Science:

  • Descriptive ‚Üí EDA
  • Inferential ‚Üí Prediction & conclusions

ü§ñ AI / ML:

  • Descriptive ‚Üí Understand data
  • Inferential ‚Üí Generalize to unseen data

Core Definitions

üîπ Descriptive Statistics
  • Collects, organizes, summarizes, and presents data
  • Does NOT predict or generalize

üëâ Focus: Given data only

üîπ Inferential Statistics
  • Draws conclusions about a population using a sample
  • Involves probability & uncertainty

üëâ Focus: Beyond the data

Key Terms

Population: Entire group
Sample: Part of population
Parameter: Population value
Statistic: Sample value

Tools Used

üìä Descriptive Statistics

  • Central Tendency: Mean, Median, Mode
  • Dispersion: Range, Variance, Standard Deviation
  • Presentation: Tables, Bar chart, Histogram, Pie chart

üìà Inferential Statistics

  • Probability & Sampling
  • Estimation & Confidence Interval
  • Hypothesis Testing (z, t, œá¬≤ tests)
  • Regression & Correlation

Population vs Sample

  • Population: Entire group we want to study
  • Sample: A smaller part taken from the population

üëâ Most statistical conclusions are made about populations using samples.

Why is it Important?

üìò Statistics: Basis of sampling & inferential statistics

üíª Data Science: Models trained on samples, applied to populations

ü§ñ AI / ML: Generalization from training data to real-world data

üéì Exams & Research: Core topic for UPSC, SSC, GATE, NET, MBA

Core Definitions

üîπ Population

Complete set of all individuals or observations

Examples:

  • All students in India
  • Entire voter list
  • All company transactions
üîπ Sample

Subset selected from the population

Examples:

  • 500 students from all students
  • One month sales from 5 years data

Key Terms

Census: Study of entire population
Sampling: Selecting a sample
Representative Sample: Reflects population
Bias: Systematic selection error

Why Use Samples?

Studying the whole population is often:

  • Expensive
  • Time-consuming
  • Impractical

üëâ Sampling is efficient and practical

Characteristics of a Good Sample

  • Randomly selected
  • Sufficient size
  • Free from bias
  • Represents population well

What is Data?

Data = raw facts, figures, or observations collected for analysis.

Main Types of Data

Data is broadly classified into:

üìã Qualitative (Categorical)
üî¢ Quantitative (Numerical)

Qualitative Data (Categorical)

  • Describes qualities or categories
  • Answers: "What type?"
  • Cannot be averaged

Examples: Gender, Blood group, Color, Department

üîπ Types of Qualitative Data

1. Nominal

  • No order
  • Examples: Gender, Blood group, Nationality

2. Ordinal

  • Ordered categories
  • Difference not measurable
  • Examples: Grades (A, B, C), Ratings (Poor ‚Üí Excellent)

Quantitative Data (Numerical)

  • Data in numbers
  • Answers: "How much / How many?"
  • Arithmetic operations are valid

Examples: Height, Weight, Marks, Salary

üîπ Types of Quantitative Data

1. Discrete

  • Countable, whole numbers
  • Examples: No. of students, cars, defects

2. Continuous

  • Measurable, any value in a range
  • Examples: Height, Weight, Time

üëâ One dataset can have multiple data types

Math Applicability

‚úî Quantitative: Mean, median, etc. possible

Example: Marks ‚Üí Mean possible

‚ùå Qualitative: No averaging

Example: Gender ‚Üí Mean not possible

Easy Identification Trick

Question Asked Data Type

üëâ Quantitative = Numbers üî¢

üìä Data Science Encoding vs Scaling
ü§ñ ML Categorical handling vs Numerical modeling

üéì Exams Choosing correct charts & tests

Common Mistakes

‚ùå Treating ordinal as numerical

‚úî Order ‚â† equal difference

‚ùå Averaging categorical data

‚úî Only numerical data can be averaged

‚ùå Confusing discrete & continuous

‚úî Counting = Discrete, Measuring = Continuous

‚ùå Ignoring data types in ML

‚úî Leads to wrong model results
